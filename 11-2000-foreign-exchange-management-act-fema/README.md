# Historical Background

Before FEMA, India operated under:
# FERA (Foreign Exchange Regulation Act), 1973

FERA was a highly restrictive law.

Even minor foreign exchange violations were treated as:
- criminal offenses,
- non-bailable violations,
- and serious legal crimes.

The system created:
- fear among businesses,
- low global integration,
- and barriers to international trade.

After economic liberalization, the Government introduced:
# Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999

which officially came into force on:
# 1 June 2000

FEMA permanently replaced FERA and transformed India's foreign exchange philosophy from:
- "control"
to:
- "management"

# 10 Master Key Points

---

# 1️⃣ FERA's Restrictive Legacy

FERA treated foreign exchange violations as criminal offenses.

## Problems
- Fear-based enforcement
- Business restrictions
- Low global integration

---

# 2️⃣ Inspector Raj & Presumption of Guilt

Under FERA:
- businesses were presumed guilty,
- heavy compliance pressure existed,
- foreign exchange activity was tightly controlled.

---

# 3️⃣ Historic Introduction of FEMA

The Government passed:
# FEMA, 1999

which officially came into force on:
# 1 June 2000

FERA was permanently abolished.

---

# 4️⃣ Decriminalization of Forex Violations

FEMA transformed forex violations from:
- criminal offenses
to:
- civil matters.

## Impact
- Reduced fear among businesses
- Improved investment confidence

---

# 5️⃣ Shift from Regulation to Management

FERA focused on:
- controlling foreign exchange

FEMA focused on:
- managing and facilitating forex flows.

This reflected a major change in economic philosophy.

---

# 6️⃣ Oxygen for India's IT Sector

FEMA simplified international financial operations for:
- TCS
- Infosys
- Wipro

## Impact
- Easier overseas business
- Faster dollar inflows
- Expansion of IT exports

---

# 7️⃣ Increased Confidence of Multinational Companies

Global companies gained confidence in India's financial system.

## Impact
- Higher FDI inflows
- Increased foreign participation
- Better global integration

---

# 8️⃣ Current Account Liberalization

Indian citizens gained easier access to:
- foreign travel,
- overseas education,
- medical payments,
- and foreign currency exchange.

---

# 9️⃣ Reorganization of Enforcement Directorate (ED)

The role of ED shifted toward:
- monitoring illegal forex transactions,
- hawala investigations,
- and money laundering cases.
