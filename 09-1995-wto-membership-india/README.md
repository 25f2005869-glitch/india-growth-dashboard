---

# Historical Background

India was already a member of:
# GATT (General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade)

since 1948.

After the:
# Marrakesh Agreement (1994)

GATT was transformed into:
# WTO (World Trade Organization)

India became a founding WTO member on:
# 1 January 1995

This marked India's transition toward:
- globalization,
- open markets,
- and international trade integration.

# 10 Master Key Points

---

# 1️⃣ GATT to WTO Transition

India transitioned from GATT membership to WTO membership in 1995.

## Impact
- Global trade integration
- International market participation
- Multilateral trade framework

---

# 2️⃣ Most Favored Nation (MFN) Status

WTO membership provided India with:
# Most Favored Nation (MFN)

trade protection.

## Impact
- Non-discriminatory trade access
- Better export opportunities

---

# 3️⃣ Tariff Reduction

India gradually reduced:
- customs duties,
- import tariffs,
- and trade barriers.

## Impact
- Open market economy
- Cheaper imports
- Industrial competition

---

# 4️⃣ End of Quantitative Restrictions

India removed:
- quota systems,
- import restrictions,
- quantity controls.

## Impact
- Free-market trade environment
- Liberalized imports and exports

---

# 5️⃣ TRIPS Agreement & Intellectual Property

WTO required India to modernize:
- patent laws,
- copyright systems,
- intellectual property regulations.

## Impact
- Product patent system introduced
- Global compliance achieved

---

# 6️⃣ Rise of Indian Pharmaceutical Exports

Indian pharmaceutical companies expanded globally.

## Impact
- Generic medicine exports increased
- India became a global pharma hub

---

# 7️⃣ Textile Export Expansion

The removal of:
# Multi-Fibre Arrangement (MFA)

quotas increased opportunities for Indian textile exports.

---

# 8️⃣ TRIMS Agreement & Foreign Investment

WTO improved transparency for:
- foreign investors,
- multinational corporations,
- global manufacturing.

## Impact
- Increased FDI confidence
- Better investment environment

---

# 9️⃣ Anti-Dumping Protection

WTO rules allowed India to impose:
# Anti-Dumping Duties

against unfair foreign pricing practices.

## Impact
- Domestic industry protection
- Fair trade mechanisms
