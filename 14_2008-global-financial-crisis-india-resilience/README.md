# 🏛 Historical Background

In September 2008:

# Lehman Brothers

one of America's oldest investment banks collapsed suddenly.

This triggered:

- global panic,
- stock market crashes,
- banking failures,
- financial instability,
- and worldwide recession.

While many developed countries entered deep economic collapse:

# India remained surprisingly resilient.

India's banking system survived because of:

- strong RBI regulations,
- conservative banking norms,
- liquidity protections,
- and strong domestic demand.

This became one of the most important examples of:

# Macroeconomic Stability & Crisis Management

in modern economic history.

---

# 🔥 10 Master Key Points

---

# 1️⃣ Lehman Brothers Collapse (September 2008)

The collapse of:

# Lehman Brothers

triggered:

- global financial panic,
- stock market crashes,
- banking instability,
- and the Great Recession.

Financial markets across the world collapsed rapidly.

---

# 2️⃣ FII Capital Shock

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) suddenly withdrew:

# More than $12 Billion

from Indian financial markets.

As a result:

- Sensex crashed over 50%
- Investor fear increased
- Market volatility exploded

---

# 3️⃣ Indian Banking System Stability

Unlike America and Europe:

# No Indian Bank Collapsed

This included:

- Public sector banks
- HDFC Bank
- ICICI Bank
- SBI

India's banking sector remained stable because of:

- strict RBI supervision,
- controlled risk exposure,
- and conservative banking policies.

---

# 4️⃣ Ban on Toxic Financial Products

RBI never allowed Indian banks to aggressively participate in:

- subprime mortgage speculation,
- toxic derivative products,
- risky credit-default systems.

This protected India's core banking system from collapse.

---

# 5️⃣ High CRR & SLR Protection

Indian banks were legally required to maintain:

- CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio)
- SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio)

This forced banks to keep large liquidity reserves.

As a result:

# Bank Runs Never Happened

during the crisis.

---

# 6️⃣ RBI Liquidity Infusion

As the crisis deepened:

RBI rapidly reduced:

- Repo Rate
- CRR
- interest rates

to inject liquidity into markets.

## Repo Rate Reduction

From:

# 9%

to:

# 4.75%

This prevented:

- liquidity crunch,
- credit collapse,
- and banking panic.

---

# 7️⃣ Strength of Domestic Consumption

India's economy was not fully dependent on exports.

Strong domestic demand came from:

- middle-class consumption,
- rural demand,
- infrastructure spending,
- and internal markets.

This reduced recession pressure.

---

# 8️⃣ Government Fiscal Stimulus

The Government introduced:

- excise duty cuts,
- infrastructure spending,
- economic stimulus packages.

This supported:

- employment,
- industrial demand,
- and economic growth.

---

# 9️⃣ V-Shaped GDP Recovery

While many developed countries entered negative GDP growth:

India rapidly recovered.

## GDP Growth

### 2008–09:
# 6.7%

### 2009–10:
# 8.6%

This became one of the strongest:

# V-Shaped Recoveries

in the world.
