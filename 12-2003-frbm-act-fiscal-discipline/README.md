# Historical Background

After the 1991 economic crisis, India still struggled with:
- high fiscal deficits,
- rising public debt,
- and excessive government borrowing.

Governments often financed expenditure through:
- borrowing,
- monetization,
- and deficit financing.

This threatened:
- inflation stability,
- investor confidence,
- and long-term economic sustainability.

To restore fiscal discipline, the Government passed:
# Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management Act (FRBM), 2003

under Prime Minister:
# Atal Bihari Vajpayee

The FRBM Act aimed to:
- reduce fiscal deficits,
- improve budget transparency,
- stabilize public debt,
- and strengthen macroeconomic governance.

# 10 Master Key Points

---

# 1️⃣ Debt Crisis & Fiscal Pressure

Even after liberalization, India continued facing:
- high fiscal deficits,
- growing debt,
- and macroeconomic instability.

---

# 2️⃣ Historic Approval of FRBM Act (2003)

The Vajpayee Government passed:
# FRBM Act, 2003

to enforce fiscal discipline and responsible budgeting.

---

# 3️⃣ Fiscal Deficit Rule (3% Target)

The law aimed to reduce:
# Fiscal Deficit ≤ 3% of GDP

This became India's most important fiscal rule.

---

# 4️⃣ Revenue Deficit Reduction

The FRBM framework aimed to eliminate:
# Revenue Deficit

meaning:
- borrowing should fund development,
- not routine government expenses.

---

# 5️⃣ Restriction on Deficit Financing

The Act restricted:
- direct monetization of government deficits,
- excessive money printing through RBI financing.

## Impact
- Better inflation control
- Monetary discipline

---

# 6️⃣ Fiscal Transparency Framework

The government became legally required to present:
- Medium-Term Fiscal Policy Statements
- Fiscal Policy Strategy Statements
- Macroeconomic Framework Reports

## Impact
- Budget transparency increased
- Accountability improved

---

# 7️⃣ Sovereign Credit Rating Improvement

Global rating agencies observed improved fiscal discipline.

## Impact
- Better sovereign ratings
- Higher FDI inflows
- Increased investor confidence

---

# 8️⃣ Escape Clause Provision

The Act allowed temporary relaxation during:
- war,
- national emergencies,
- natural disasters,
- economic crises.

Example:
# COVID-19 pandemic flexibility

---

# 9️⃣ N.K. Singh Committee Reforms (2016)

The FRBM framework was later updated.

## Target
Combined central and state debt:
# ≤ 60% of GDP
