# Project Overview

- delayed transactions,

- trading volume growth,

# Historical Background

Before 1992, the Indian stock market was dominated by the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE).

The market suffered from:
- broker monopoly,
- manual trading systems,
- lack of transparency,
- settlement delays,
- and high manipulation risk.

After the 1992 financial reforms and the Harshad Mehta Scam, the Government of India introduced major capital market modernization reforms.

In November 1992, the **National Stock Exchange (NSE)** was established based on the recommendations of the **Pherwani Committee**.

NSE introduced:
- electronic trading,
- nationwide connectivity,
- anonymous order matching,
- and modern settlement systems.

These reforms completely transformed India's stock market ecosystem.

# 10 Master Key Reform Points

---

# 1️⃣ Challenge to BSE Monopoly

Before NSE, BSE had near-complete dominance over Indian stock market trading.

A small group of brokers controlled market access and trading activities.

## Impact of NSE
- Increased competition
- Reduced broker dominance
- Democratized market participation

---

# 2️⃣ Establishment of NSE (1992)

The National Stock Exchange was established in November 1992.

It became India's first fully demutualized exchange.

## Importance
- Ownership and trading rights separated
- Reduced conflict of interest
- Improved governance structure

---

# 3️⃣ Screen-Based Trading System (NEAT)

In 1994, NSE launched the:
## NEAT (National Exchange for Automated Trading) System

This replaced:
- open outcry trading,
- paper slips,
- and manual order execution.

## Impact
- Faster transactions
- Electronic order matching
- Reduced manipulation

---

# 4️⃣ Nationwide Satellite Network (V-SAT Technology)

NSE introduced V-SAT satellite communication technology across India.

## Impact
- Nationwide real-time trading
- Equal access to market prices
- Trading democratization

Even small-city brokers could trade instantly at national market prices.

---

# 5️⃣ Settlement Cycle Reduction

Before NSE:
- settlement cycles took 14–30 days

After NSE:
- settlement cycles reduced significantly

## Impact
- Faster transactions
- Reduced fraud
- Higher efficiency

---

# 6️⃣ Anonymous Order Matching

NSE introduced anonymous electronic order matching.

The computer system matched orders only on:
- best price,
- and time priority.

## Impact
- Reduced broker manipulation
- Fairer trading environment
- Higher transparency

---

# 7️⃣ NSCCL Clearing Corporation (1995)

NSE established:
## National Securities Clearing Corporation Limited (NSCCL)

## Importance
- Guaranteed trade settlement
- Reduced counterparty risk
- Improved trust in markets

---

# 8️⃣ Massive Growth in Trading Volume

Within a short period, NSE trading volume surpassed older exchanges.

## Impact
- Increased participation
- Higher market liquidity
- Faster capital market growth

---

# 9️⃣ Increased Confidence of Foreign Investors

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) preferred transparent electronic systems.

## Impact
- Higher foreign capital inflow
- International market confidence
- Global integration of Indian markets
